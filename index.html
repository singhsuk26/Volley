How it works in Salesforce

Both sides of the net

The Volley is built on two Salesforce objects and a bidirectional Flow architecture. Neither side can spam — every ping goes through the matching engine first.

Org
📋
Org posts a role
Volunteer Job record created. VolleyMatchingService runs automatically and scores all Active volunteers. Threshold: 80+ score required before any ping is sent.
Both
🎯
Engine finds your match
VolleyPing__c record created for volunteers above threshold. Favourite-charity volunteers are flagged Priority = true and pinged first via Experience Cloud notification.
Org
📣
Org can also message
Premium-tier orgs can send one personalised message per matched volunteer per role (rate-limited by Flow). Message stored in VolleyMessage__c — never email scraped.
Volunteer
↔
Volunteer volleys back
One tap in the portal or mobile app. Flow creates Volunteer Hours (Applied), sends confirmation to both sides, and clears the ping queue for this role.
Both
🔒
Safeguarding always wins
Pings are blocked if DBS is required but not cleared. Orgs cannot see volunteer PII until confirmed. Feedback flags go to Volley safeguarding team, not the org.
Volunteer
⭐
Both sides leave feedback
Post-task Scheduled Flow triggers feedback forms for both volunteer and org. WouldWorkAgain from both sides is the key quality signal — it's a two-way rating.

⚠ Honest about what we can and cannot measure

Output metrics (volunteer hours, sign-ups, repeat engagement) tell us about reach and activity. They cannot, on their own, tell us whether communities are better off. That is why Volley's M&E framework pairs every output with an outcome indicator — collected through post-task surveys, annual org surveys, and platform retention data — and why we track % of organisations reporting volunteers enabled additional service delivery as our primary impact measure, not volunteer hours.
